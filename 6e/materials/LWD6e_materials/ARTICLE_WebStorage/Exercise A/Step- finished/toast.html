How to Make the Best Toast

  1. Choose Quality Bread:
    Start with fresh, high-quality bread. A thicker slice gives a crunch outside and softness inside, while thinner slices crisp up evenly.
  2. Prep Your Bread:
    If desired, lightly brush both sides with a bit of butter or olive oil for extra flavor and crispness.
  3. Toast It:
    • Using a Toaster: Set it to medium heat. Toast until golden brown and crisp.
    • Using an Oven: Preheat to 375°F (190°C). Place bread on a baking sheet and bake for 5–7 minutes, flipping halfway.
    • Using a Pan: Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add butter/oil and toast each side until golden and crunchy.
  4. Customize Your Crunch:
    Adjust the toasting time based on your preference—lightly golden for a soft bite, or darker for extra crispiness.
  5. Add Toppings:
    While warm, spread butter to let it melt, or top with avocado, jam, honey, or any of your favorite toppings. For savory toast, add a pinch of salt, pepper, or chili flakes.
  6. Enjoy Immediately:
    Toast is best enjoyed fresh when it’s warm and crispy.
